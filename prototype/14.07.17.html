Various – Pop Makossa

2017 Analog Africa

Analog Africa smashing it as usual. Another brill compilation focusing on another hybridic African scene, this time Cameroonian dance music in the late 70s & early 80s. Everything on here is a banger. There’s all sorts of jams with acute guitars and cosmic synths shredding everywhere. The rhythms feel urgent and are tied to wickedly tight basslines. This compilation will make you move body parts. It collects a range of styles and tempos and still feels cohesive, much like recent favourites Space Echo & Digital Zandoli. Highlights include Nen Lambo, Africa and Sekele Movement. Bonus points for that lively cover.

Robert Palmer – Clues

1980 Island

Yep, this is by that guy). It wins best recent surprise. Skip the couple of weaker tracks and it’s charming; full of 80s hustle, new-wave tinged pop and a decent Gary Numan cover. There’s also Woke Up Laughing, a delightful overlappy ballad that feels familiar on first listen.

Linear Movement – On The Screen

1007 Minimal Wave (reissue)

Many of the tunes on this were meant to be released around ‘83. For whatever reason they didn’t see the light of day, and it was up to reissue label Minimal Wave to find, package and release these babies up. Linear Movement was a solo side project from Peter Bonne, whose main gig was in the more experimental groups Autumn & Twilight Ritual. Gloriously catchy synthwave jams, they shimmer and crackle like an old neon sign. The yearning vocals add a sober reminder that the future may not be so bright.

Porter Ricks – Anguilla Electrica

2017 Tresor

After the subterranean duo reunited with Shadow Boat last year it was exciting to know an album was in the works. It follows on from that EP nicely; one foot in the dub sound they helped to pioneer, the other firmly in todays technical, hi-fidelity approach to sound design. The pressure and depth Porter Ricks create with percussion is brilliantly immersive. Zero filler, just six crushing tunes. Headphones a must for this one; lie on the floor and gaze upwards as you feel yourself pulled beneath the surface.

"Om" Alec Khaoli – Say You Love Me

2017 Awesome Tapes From Africa (repress)

Can’t get enough of South African jams recently. This long-awaited repress features four sticky sweet synthy bubblegum tunes. A set of beach-dance jams that play as the sun rises after an all-nighter. Mr “Om” is a bassist and you can tell. The intro that leads to that bassline on the title track; sublime! He played in Umoja whose brilliant 707 also gets a re-release. Summertime!
